Right Eye

Understanding +/-

**Important Reminder:** Avoid the common mistake of selecting a "+" value instead of a "-" value or vice versa when entering prescriptions online. Double-check your prescription details to ensure the correct value is chosen.

Understanding Sphere (SPH)

The Sphere (SPH) value on your prescription indicates the lens power required, typically in increments of 0.25 (e.g., -0.25, -0.50, -0.75, -1.00, -1.25). A "+" or "−" sign will denote whether it’s for farsightedness or nearsightedness, respectively. Opticians often place a dash above the value for a minus sign or add a "+" next to positive values.

If your SPH values exceed +6.00 or fall below -6.00, we can often still create your glasses, but a surcharge might apply. If your prescription is beyond this range, please call us at 0151 632 6611.

Occasionally, your SPH might include terms like "Plano," "Infinity," or symbols such as "~" or "∞," which all signify a value of 0.00.

Understanding Cylinder (CYL)

The Cylinder (CYL) value is specified in increments of 0.25 and can be either positive (+) or negative (-), such as -0.25, -0.50, -0.75, -1.00, -1.25, and so forth. Opticians typically write a dash above the value for a negative sign or a "+" next to positive values.

If your CYL value exceeds +3.00 or is below -3.00, we can often still manufacture your glasses, but a surcharge may apply. If your prescription is beyond this range, please contact us at 0151 632 6611.

If a CYL value is not specified for one or both eyes, it means there is no astigmatism present and no CYL correction is required. In such cases, there will be no AXIS value for the respective eye(s).

Sometimes, when no CYL correction is needed, the optician may write "DS" (Dioptre Spheres) in the CYL field, indicating that no CYL correction is required. In this case, you can leave the CYL option blank.

Understanding AXIS

"AXIS" indicates the required orientation for astigmatism correction (CYL), ranging from 0 to 180. This value is only necessary if there's a CYL value; otherwise, no AXIS is required. It ensures proper alignment of the cylindrical correction for effective astigmatism treatment.

Occasionally, prescriptions may include fractional AXIS values (e.g., 95.5) not matching standard form options. If so, round up and select the next whole number.

Understanding Inter Add

If you need single-vision glasses specifically for intermediate use, obtain your "Intermediate Addition" (Inter Add) value from your optician. This value helps create single-vision lenses for computer work and other intermediate activities, focusing at a distance beyond regular reading glasses.

For varifocal lenses, the "Inter Add" value is not required, as it is already incorporated into the lens.

If your prescription includes an "Inter Add" value, be sure to add it to the form for accurate lens preparation. This prescription will also be saved to your account for future reference.
