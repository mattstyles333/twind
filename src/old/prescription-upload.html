Frequently Asked Questions: Prescriptions

  • Your prescription's SPH indicates correction for either nearsightedness (-) or farsightedness (+). The CYL addresses astigmatism, while the Axis denotes its angle. Don't overlook your Pupillary Distance (PD) as it's essential for ensuring your lenses align with your eyes correctly. If you're ever unsure, it's best to consult an eye care professional for advice.

  • Your optician should've provided your glasses prescription after your last test. If lost, you can request another copy. You can order glasses without your prescription by selecting "send prescription later" after you have chosen your lenses.

  • Click the top right of the website, log in, and select "My Account." Then, choose "My Prescriptions."
