Glasses & Prescriptions

The best way to find your ideal frame size is to check the measurements on your current glasses, which are often printed inside the temple. Look for three numbers, typically formatted like "52â–¡18-140". The first number is the lens width, the second is the bridge width, and the third is the temple length. We recommend choosing new frames with measurements close to your current comfortable glasses.

Yes, for prescription eyewear, you'll need a valid prescription. Your prescription should be no more than 2 years old for adults and 1 year for children. You can upload your prescription during checkout or email it to us afterward. If you're ordering non-prescription glasses or sunglasses, no prescription is needed.

Your prescription contains several measurements that determine your vision correction needs:

  • SPH (Sphere): This indicates your degree of nearsightedness or farsightedness.
  • CYL (Cylinder): This measures astigmatism, an imperfection in the curvature of your eye.
  • AXIS: This specifies the orientation of astigmatism, measured in degrees.
  • ADD: This is the additional magnifying power needed for reading, typically for bifocal or progressive lenses.
  • PD (Pupillary Distance): The distance between your pupils, crucial for properly centering your lenses.

If you're unsure about your prescription, our optical experts are happy to help explain what each value means for your vision.

We offer a comprehensive range of lens options to suit various needs:

  • Single Vision: For correcting either distance or near vision.
  • Bifocal: Contains two distinct optical powers for distance and near vision.
  • Varifocal/Progressive: Seamlessly transitions between multiple prescriptions.
  • Blue Light Filtering: Reduces eye strain from digital screens.
  • Photochromic: Automatically darkens in sunlight and clears indoors.
  • Polarized: Reduces glare from reflective surfaces, ideal for driving and outdoor activities.
  • High-Index: Thinner, lighter lenses for stronger prescriptions.

Blue light filtering lenses help reduce eye strain from extended screen time by filtering out harmful blue light emitted by digital devices, while allowing beneficial blue light to pass through. They may be beneficial if you spend long hours on digital devices, experience digital eye strain, or have trouble sleeping after screen time. These lenses are available with or without prescription and can be added to most of our frames during checkout.

Orders & Delivery

Production time varies depending on prescription complexity and lens type. Standard single vision lenses typically take 7-10 business days, while more complex prescriptions or specialty lenses may take 10-14 business days. Delivery times are additional to production times and depend on your location. Once your order ships, you'll receive tracking information via email.

Once your order ships, you'll receive a confirmation email with tracking information. You can also track your order by logging into your account on our website and viewing your order history. If you created an account during checkout, you can use this to track all order updates. For any specific questions about your order status, please contact our customer service team.

Yes, we offer expedited shipping options at checkout. Please note that expedited shipping affects the delivery time once your glasses are produced, but does not speed up the production process itself. For urgent orders, please contact our customer service team to discuss rush production options, which may be available for certain prescriptions and lens types at an additional cost.

We begin processing orders quickly, so changes or cancellations must be requested as soon as possible. Please contact our customer service team immediately if you need to modify or cancel your order. Changes can usually be accommodated if production hasn't begun, typically within 24 hours of order placement. Once production has started, we cannot make changes or cancel the order.

Yes, we ship to most countries worldwide. International shipping rates and delivery times vary by location and will be calculated at checkout. Please note that international orders may be subject to import duties, taxes, and customs clearance fees imposed by your country, which are the customer's responsibility. These charges are not included in our shipping costs and will be collected upon delivery.

Returns & Adjustments

We offer a 30-day satisfaction guarantee on all our eyewear. If you're not completely satisfied, contact our customer service team, and we'll help resolve the issue. This may include adjustments, replacements, or refunds as appropriate. For prescription glasses, we may request verification of the prescription to ensure accuracy. Our goal is to ensure you're completely happy with your purchase.

To initiate a return or exchange, please contact our customer service team within 30 days of receiving your order. We'll provide you with a return authorization and instructions. Please note that glasses must be returned in their original packaging and in unworn condition. For exchanges, once we receive your return, we'll process your new selection. Shipping costs for returns may apply, but exchanges typically include free shipping for the replacement item.

All our frames come with a 12-month warranty against manufacturing defects. This covers issues like loose hinges, discoloration, or peeling coatings under normal use. The warranty does not cover damage from accidents, misuse, or normal wear and tear. Lenses have a 6-month coating warranty. If you experience any issues that you believe are covered under warranty, please contact our customer service team with your order details and photos of the issue.

Yes, most glasses will need some adjustment for a perfect fit. You can visit our location for free adjustments, or any local optician who typically provides this service for a small fee. Simple adjustments like tightening screws or slightly bending the temples can often be done at home, but we recommend professional adjustments for the best fit. If you're having fit issues, our customer service team can provide guidance on adjustments or arrange for a return if necessary.

If you suspect your prescription was entered incorrectly, please contact us within 30 days of receiving your glasses. We'll ask you to send a copy of your original prescription for verification. If we find that the error was on our part, we'll remake your glasses at no cost to you. If the error was in the information you provided during ordering, we can still remake your glasses, but there may be a partial fee. We always recommend double-checking your prescription details before finalizing your order.
