How do I know which frame size is right for me?

Look for three numbers, typically formatted like "52â–¡18-140". The first number is the lens width, the second is the bridge width, and the third is the temple length. Do I need a prescription to order glasses?

You'll need a valid prescription for prescription eyewear. Your prescription should be no more than 2 years old for adults and 1 year for children. If you're ordering non-prescription glasses or sunglasses, you won't need a prescription. You can upload your prescription during checkout or email it to us afterward.

How long will it take to receive my glasses?

Production time varies depending on your prescription complexity and the type of lenses you order. Standard single vision lenses typically take 7-10 business days, while more complex prescriptions or specialty lenses may take 10-14 business days. Delivery times are additional to production times. See our delivery page for specific shipping timeframes.

What if my glasses don't fit or I'm not satisfied?

We offer a 30-day satisfaction guarantee on all our eyewear. If you're not completely satisfied, contact our customer service team, and we'll help resolve the issue. This may include adjustments, replacements, or refunds as appropriate. For prescription glasses, we may request verification of the prescription to ensure accuracy.

Do you offer blue light filtering lenses?

Yes, we offer blue light filtering lenses that help reduce eye strain from extended screen time. These lenses filter out harmful blue light emitted by digital devices while allowing beneficial blue light to pass through. They're available with or without prescription and can be added to most of our frames during the checkout process.

Lens Width

The horizontal width of each lens in millimeters.

The distance between the lenses across the nose in millimeters.

Temple Length

The length of the arms that extend to your ears in millimeters.

Understanding Frame Measurements

Choosing the right frame size is key to making sure your glasses feel comfortable and fit well. The measurements can be found on the inside of one of the arms, or sometimes engraved on the inside of the nose bridge.

These numbers typically appear in a format like 52â–¡18-140, representing:

  • Lens Width (52) The horizontal width of each lens in millimeters.
  • Bridge Width (18) The distance between the lenses across the nose.
  • Temple Length (140) The length of the arms that extend over your ears.

Finding Your Perfect Fit

When selecting new frames, consider these additional measurements for the best fit:

  • Frame Width The total width of the frame, which should align with your face width.
  • Lens Height The vertical measurement of the lens, important for bifocal or progressive lenses.
  • Face Shape Different frame styles complement different face shapes. Round faces often pair well with angular frames, while square faces may suit rounder frames.

If you're between sizes or unsure, our opticians are always available to help. Contact us for personalized recommendations.

Quick Tips for Perfect Fitting Glasses

Comfort Check

Your glasses should sit comfortably on your nose without pinching or sliding down. The temples should rest gently on your ears without pressure.

Alignment

Your eyes should be centered in the lenses. The frames shouldn't be wider than your face or rest on your cheeks when you smile.

Weight Distribution

The weight of your glasses should be evenly distributed between your nose and ears for all-day comfort.
