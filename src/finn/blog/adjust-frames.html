How to Adjust Your Glasses at Home: The Essentials

Sometimes your glasses don’t fit quite right—maybe they’re sliding down your nose or feeling too tight on the sides. While some adjustments can be made at home, we recommend being gentle and cautious to avoid damaging your frames. Here are a few simple tips to get your glasses feeling more comfortable again.

1. Glasses Sliding Down Your Nose?

If your glasses keep slipping, there are small adjustments you can make, depending on whether you have plastic or metal frames:

  • Plastic Frames: Gently bend the temple arms downwards where they curve behind your ears. This can help them sit more securely. Be very careful with the pressure you apply to avoid breaking the frames.
  • Metal Frames: You can make slight adjustments by gently bending the arms downward behind your ears as well. Additionally, for frames with nose pads, you can pinch the nose pads closer together to help them grip your nose more firmly.

2. Glasses Feeling Too Tight?

If your frames are causing pressure, it could be a sign that your glasses are too small. However, if you'd like, you can try these simple steps:

  • Plastic Frames: Carefully adjust the arms outward by gently bending them. Be cautious and avoid over-bending, as plastic can break with too much pressure.
  • Metal Frames: For metal frames, you can gently bend the temple arms outward to reduce pressure on your temples. You can also adjust the nose pads by slightly widening them to relieve pressure.

Tips for Success

  • Use Both Hands: Always put your glasses on and take them off with both hands. This keeps the frame balanced and prevents unnecessary bending.
  • Avoid Overdoing It: Small, incremental adjustments are key. Over-bending can cause more harm than good, especially with plastic frames.

Important Notes for Varifocals

If you’ve recently purchased varifocal lenses, it’s important to know that they may take some time to get used to. Adjusting the frame could impact how you see through these lenses, so we recommend allowing time for adaptation before making any adjustments.
