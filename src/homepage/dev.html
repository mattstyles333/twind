What Are Occupational Lenses?

Occupational lenses, also known as office lenses or computer glasses, are degressive lenses designed for those who need sharp intermediate and near vision throughout the day.

Similar to varifocals, they provide a smooth transition between prescriptions but focus on enhancing computer work, reading, and other close-to-mid-range tasks, rather than distance vision. By reducing the need to switch between glasses, they offer comfortable, strain-free vision, perfect for office workers, crafters, and anyone who spends extended periods at a desk or screen.

The Secrets of Occupational Lenses

Occupational lenses can make all the difference to your workday comfort and vision. But with so many options, how do you choose the right pair? Here are the key secrets to getting the best lenses for your needs.

1. Coatings Matter

  • Blue Light Protection Ideal for digital screens, reducing eye strain and improving comfort.
  • UV Protection Even indoors, UV rays can cause long-term damage. A UV coating shields your eyes.
  • Durability If you frequently put on and take off your glasses, an anti-scratch coating is essential.

2. Lens Weight & Comfort

  • The index (thickness) of your lenses affects weight. Higher index lenses are thinner and lighter perfect for all-day wear.
  • If your prescription is stronger, opting for a 1.6 or 1.67 index ensures lightweight comfort.

3. Not All Jobs Need the Same Lenses

  • Varifocals If you switch between near, intermediate, and far distances frequently.
  • Intermediate Lenses If your work is 100% computer-based, perfect for screen use.
  • Task-Specific Lenses For precision work like design or mechanics.

4. Big Brands vs. Smart Choices

  • Essilor & Zeiss Trusted names with premium lens tech.
  • Nova Lenses High-quality lenses at a better price point cutting-edge performance without the premium price.

5. Frame Depth Matters

  • Choosing a deep frame allows you to get the most out of your occupational lenses, giving you better vision coverage for different working distances.
  • The frame depth in the center of the lens, where your pupil sits, is crucial. A deeper frame ensures you get the full benefit of the lens design, allowing smooth transitions between focal points and reducing distortion. Without enough depth, your vision may feel restricted, making occupational lenses less effective.
