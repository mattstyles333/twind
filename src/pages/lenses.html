How to choose your lenses

1. Lens use

After selecting a frame for style and comfort, choose a lens type for your needs: distance, intermediate, or reading. For a versatile solution, consider bifocal or varifocal lenses. However, these are not recommended best for sports frames due to possible visual shifts during active use. If you have any problems or queries then get in contact with our optical service advisors.

2. Choose your Lens Index

What exactly is the lens index? It's a measure that indicates the thickness or thinness of eyeglass lenses, determining how they bend light. At Spex4less, we offer an extensive range of lens index options to cater to a variety of needs. Our selection starts with the standard 1.5 lens, a popular choice for many. For those seeking more refined profiles, we provide the 1.59, 1.6, and 1.67 lenses, and for our slimmest lens option, we have the ultra-thin 1.74 lens. However, it's crucial to mention that not all frames are compatible with every lens index. For instance, rimless glasses are not the best fit for the 1.5 or 1.74 index lenses.

3. Pick your coatings

Lens coatings are specialised treatments that add a protective layer to your lenses. These coatings not only enhance your visual experience but also amplify the durability and functionality of your glasses. The most suitable coating often depends on individual lifestyles. For instance, if you spend considerable time in front of screens, a blue light coating can be beneficial to filter out harmful blue light emissions. Additionally, consider our premium options: our Honeycomb coating offers scratch resistance, UV protection, and is specifically made to alleviate eye strain from prolonged screen use.

4. Photochromic, Tints and Mirrors

Photochromic, mirrored and tinted lenses provide a range of customization options for individuals seeking to enhance their sunglass experience. Each of these lens choices boasts distinct characteristics, tailoring to various needs and aesthetic preferences. In addition to the fundamental UV protection they offer, these lenses introduce an element of style that can elevate the overall appearance of your glasses.

5. Enter your prescription

Once you have got your prescription, you'll be better positioned to determine the optimal lens index for your needs. If you have any queries about your prescription, please get in touch with us at 0151 632 6611.
