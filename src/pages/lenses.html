How to choose your lenses

1. Lens use

After selecting a frame for style and comfort, choose a lens type for your needs: distance, intermediate, or reading. Bifocals or varifocals offer versatility but may not suit sports frames. For help, contact our optical service advisors.

2. Choose your Lens Index

The lens index denotes lens thickness, impacting light refraction. Spex4less offers indexes from the standard 1.5 to the ultra-thin 1.74. Note that thicker or thinner lenses (like 1.5 and 1.74) may not fit all frame types, such as rimless and wraparound designs. For more information on lens indexâ€™s check our recommended lens index table.

3. Pick your coatings

Lens coatings add protection and enhance lens performance. The right coating varies with your lifestyle; for example, a blue light coating is good for screen-heavy routines. Our premium Honeycomb coating provides scratch resistance, UV protection, and reduces eye strain from extended screen time.

4. Photochromic, Tints and Mirrors

Photochromic, mirrored and tinted lenses offer customization for enhanced sunglass function and style, each with unique features to meet different needs and tastes, all while providing essential UV protection.

5. Enter your prescription

Once you have got your prescription, you'll be better positioned to determine the optimal lens index for your needs. If you have any queries about your prescription, please get in touch with us at 0151 632 6611.
