Why Choose Single Vision Glasses?

When you require glasses for a singular vision purpose, your optometrist might suggest single-vision eyewear. Typically, individuals below the age of 40 opt for these lenses, as they often have just one specific visual need.

However, if you experience presbyopia, it might be necessary to have distinct glasses for different tasks â€” one for distant activities like driving and another for close-up tasks such as reading.

Understanding Single Vision Lenses

Single vision implies that the lens provides one specific focal length. For instance, if watching TV from your couch seems blurry because of the room's length, glasses with a single-vision lens for distance would be necessary for a clearer view.

Similarly, if reading a book seems out of focus, glasses with a single-vision lens catered to near-distance can enhance the clarity of your close-range vision.
