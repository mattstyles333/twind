Lens Coatings

It's hard enough choosing the right frames for your glasses, and then you have to decide which lens coatings you need too!

Lens coatings are a treatment that provides additional protection for lenses. They help you to experience enhanced vision and make the most out of your new glasses. Lens coatings can improve the durability, performance and appearance of all types of lenses.

The coating you choose will depend on your individual visual needs and lifestyle. So dive into our handy guide and learn more about the advantages of each lens coating, so you can choose the right one for your requirements.

Anti-Reflective Coating

This coating eliminates surface reflections on a lens, enabling more light transmission, enhancing vision, reducing eye strain, and improving the aesthetics of glasses. An anti-reflective coat also enhances driver safety by minimizing glare from streetlights or headlights, leading to faster recognition of road hazards, especially at night.

Blue-light Coating

Blue light exists in both natural daylight and electronic devices like computers, smartphones, TVs, and strip lights. With its short wavelength, blue light easily enters the eyes, leading to potential issues like retinal damage, eye strain, and fatigue.

UV and Durable+ Coating

Our UV and Durable+ coating offers a combination of UV protection and scratch resistance. Harmful UV rays from sunlight are countered by this coating, enhancing eye safety. Moreover, it includes a layer that resists scratches, maintaining the lens quality amid everyday use.

Anti-Fogging Coating

An anti-fog coating absorbs moisture from warm air on lenses, which helps prevent them from fogging up. This lens coating is particularly useful for keeping your glasses clear and steam-free when you are wearing a mask, drinking a hot drink, cooking, or taking part in outdoor activities.

Honeycomb Coating

Introducing honeycomb lenses: Reduce glare by redirecting light from all angles. Advanced anti-reflective coating enhances night driving, minimizes fatigue, and provides UV protection. Sharper vision, water/oil repellence, low maintenance. Ideal for low-light, combating fatigue, enhancing clarity for safer nighttime driving.

Photochromic Coating

Photochromic lenses adjust their color based on atmospheric light brightness. They darken with increased UV exposure. This coating suits those frequently transitioning between indoor and outdoor settings. However, inside a vehicle with lower UV, their darkening ability decreases, so they aren't suitable for driving.
